5 Signs You Need to Rewire Your Home

By SparkConnect Editorial • 4 min read • July 2026
Back to Home

Electrical wiring doesn't last forever. In Nigeria, where many homes were built decades ago — and some were wired informally during construction — outdated or faulty wiring is one of the leading causes of house fires and electrical hazards. The trouble is, you often can't see the problem until it becomes an emergency.

Knowing the warning signs can save your family, your appliances, and your property. Here are 5 key indicators that it's time to call a verified electrician on SparkConnect and get your home rewired.

The 5 Warning Signs

1

Frequently Tripping Circuit Breakers

If your circuit breakers trip regularly — especially when you use multiple appliances at once — it means your wiring can't handle the electrical load of your modern home. While a single trip is normal, repeated tripping is your circuit breaker's way of saying "the wiring is struggling." This is especially common in older homes where the wiring was designed for far fewer electrical devices than we use today.

2

Flickering or Dimming Lights

Lights that flicker or dim when you turn on the kettle, washing machine, or AC unit are a sign of overloaded or loose wiring. While a single flickering bulb might just need replacing, flickering across multiple rooms or in sync with appliance use points to a deeper wiring problem that needs immediate attention from a professional electrician.

3

Burning Smell or Scorch Marks Near Outlets

A burning smell — even a faint one — coming from your walls, outlets, or switch plates is a serious red flag. Scorch marks or discolouration around sockets or plug points indicate that wires have already started arcing or melting behind the walls. Do not ignore this. Turn off the affected circuit at your breaker and call an electrician immediately. This is a fire risk.

4

Outlets That Spark, Feel Warm, or Don't Work

Healthy electrical outlets should be cool to the touch and deliver consistent power. If you notice outlets that spark when you plug something in, feel warm or hot, or that have stopped working entirely, these are signs of faulty connections or damaged wiring. Using such outlets is dangerous and risks electrocution or fire.

5

Your Home Is More Than 20–30 Years Old

If your building was constructed in the 1980s, 1990s, or early 2000s and hasn't had a full electrical inspection since, it's likely overdue. Older wiring insulation degrades over time, older systems may not be grounded properly, and original circuits weren't designed for today's power demand from air conditioners, inverters, and multiple electronic devices. A professional rewire protects your investment long-term.

What You Should Do Next

If you're seeing any of the above signs, don't wait for a fire or electric shock to force your hand. The cost of rewiring is a fraction of what you'd pay to deal with the aftermath of an electrical fire. More importantly, it could save a life.
