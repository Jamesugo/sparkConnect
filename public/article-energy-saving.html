10 Ways to Cut Your Electricity Consumption in Nigeria

By SparkConnect Editorial•6 min read•March 2027
Back to Home

With rising tariffs across the country, keeping your energy bills low requires more than just turning off the lights when you leave a room. Here are the most effective ways to lower your monthly consumption.

Switch to Inverter ACs

Air conditioners are the biggest power hogs in most Nigerian homes. Standard ACs use massive amounts of energy when the compressor cycles on and off. Inverter ACs smoothly adjust their speed, saving up to 50% on electricity.

Unplug Phantom Loads

TVs, microwaves, and chargers still consume electricity when plugged in but not in use. This "phantom load" or "vampire energy" can add up to 10% of your bill. Always switch off at the socket.
