How to Choose the Right Electrician for Your Business

By SparkConnect Editorial • 5 min read • July 2026
Back to Home

Hiring an electrician for your business is a decision that carries far more weight than a home repair. Commercial and industrial electrical systems are more complex, operate at higher voltages, power critical business equipment, and must comply with safety standards. Getting it wrong can mean equipment damage, employee safety risks, regulatory violations — or worse.

Whether you run a small shop, a restaurant, a warehouse, or a growing company, here's exactly what to look for when selecting an electrician for your commercial property in Nigeria.

Why Commercial is Different from Residential

A residential electrician is trained to handle household wiring — typically single-phase systems, plug sockets, lighting circuits, and consumer units. Commercial electrical work operates at a different level entirely:

  • Three-phase power systems serving heavy machinery
  • Industrial-grade switchgear and distribution boards
  • Emergency lighting, fire alarm integration, and access control wiring
  • Data and communications cabling for modern offices
  • Compliance with commercial building electrical codes

This means you should always specifically seek out an electrician with commercial or industrial experience — not just anyone who wires homes.

7 Things to Check Before Hiring

Verified Experience in Commercial Work

Ask specifically whether they have handled commercial installations, not just homes. A professional with experience in shops, offices, or factories will understand the demands of a business environment — downtime costs money, so they must work efficiently and accurately.

Knowledge of Three-Phase Systems

Most commercial and industrial premises use three-phase power. Your electrician must be proficient in installing, maintaining, and troubleshooting three-phase distribution boards, motors, and equipment. Always verify this during your initial conversation.

Strong Past Client Reviews

Check their ratings and read reviews on their SparkConnect profile. Look specifically for reviews from other business owners — someone praised for fixing household outlets is not the same as someone trusted by a factory floor or a busy restaurant kitchen.

Ability to Provide a Detailed Written Quote

A professional commercial electrician will always provide an itemised quote — materials, labour, timeline, and any likely additional costs. If someone can only give you a vague verbal estimate, that's a red flag. A written quote protects both parties and sets clear expectations.

Insurance and Liability Coverage

If an electrician damages your equipment or injures someone on your premises, you need to know who bears that cost. Ask whether they carry any form of liability cover. While this is still developing in the Nigerian market, responsible professionals will take ownership of their work.

Clear Communication and Professionalism

Do they respond promptly? Do they explain what needs to be done in clear terms? Do they show up when scheduled? Professionalism during the hiring process almost always mirrors how they'll behave on the job. Poor communication before work starts is a strong predictor of problems during and after.

Availability for Ongoing Maintenance

Commercial electrical systems need periodic maintenance — not just a one-off fix. The best commercial electricians will want to build a long-term relationship with your business, understanding your systems over time. Find someone interested in your ongoing needs, not just a quick job.

⚠️ Red Flag Alert: Beware of electricians who quote extremely low prices without seeing your premises, refuse to put anything in writing, or pressure you into starting work immediately. In commercial electrical work, cutting corners can be catastrophic and extremely expensive to fix.

Questions to Ask Before You Hire

  • Have you worked on commercial properties similar to mine before?
  • Can you provide references from previous business clients?
  • Are you familiar with three-phase power systems?
  • Can you provide a written, itemised quote before starting?
  • How long will the work take and will there be any downtime?
  • Are you available for follow-up maintenance and emergency callouts?
