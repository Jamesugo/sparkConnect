Understanding Your Electricity Bill in Nigeria

By SparkConnect Editorial•5 min read•January 2027
Back to Home

Whether you use a prepaid meter or receive estimated billing, understanding your electricity bill is the first step to taking control of your energy expenses.

What is a Tariff Band?

In Nigeria, areas are grouped into Tariff Bands (Band A, B, C, D, and E) based on the average hours of electricity supplied per day. Band A customers receive the most power (and pay the highest rate per unit), while Band E receives the least.

Prepaid vs. Postpaid Meters

Prepaid meters allow you to pay exactly for the units (kWh) you consume, protecting you from "crazy bills" or arbitrary estimated billing. If you are still on estimated billing, you have the right to request a meter from your DisCo.
