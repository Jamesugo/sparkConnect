Have you ever felt a mild shock when touching a metal appliance like a washing machine or refrigerator? That is a classic sign of poor or non-existent earthing in your home.

What is Earthing?

Earthing (or grounding) is a safety system that provides a direct, low-resistance path for excess electricity to travel harmlessly into the ground. If a wire becomes loose and touches the metal casing of an appliance, the earth wire channels the current away, preventing you from being electrocuted.

Why Many Homes Lack Proper Earthing

During construction, some builders cut costs by using substandard earthing rods, failing to bury them deeply enough, or ignoring the earth wire entirely in the sockets. This puts everyone in the building at severe risk.
