What to Do During an Electrical Emergency at Home

By SparkConnect Editorial•3 min read•September 2026
Back to Home

Electrical emergencies can escalate quickly. Whether it's a sparking outlet, a burning smell, or a power surge, knowing how to react can prevent fires and save lives.

Step 1: Turn Off the Main Power

If you suspect an electrical emergency, your first action should be to turn off the main power supply at the distribution board (changeover or breaker panel). Do not touch any appliances or wires.

Step 2: Never Use Water on an Electrical Fire

If a fire has started, never try to extinguish it with water, as water conducts electricity. Use a Class C fire extinguisher. If one is not available, evacuate immediately and call emergency services.
